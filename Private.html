Private Pilot License (PPL) Requirements Under 14 CFR Part 61

Ground Training Requirements

Aeronautical Knowledge Instruction

Complete ground training with an authorized instructor or through a home-study course.

Topics to Cover 14 CFR 61.105 :

  • Federal Aviation Regulations (FARs): Understanding the rules governing aviation activities.
  • Aerodynamics: Principles of flight and how aircraft fly.
  • Aircraft Systems: Familiarity with engine, electrical, fuel, and hydraulic systems.
  • Flight Instruments: Reading and interpreting instrument data.
  • Navigation: Use of aeronautical charts, navigation systems, and flight planning.
  • Weather Theory and Reports: Identifying and interpreting weather patterns and forecasts.
  • Radio Communication Procedures: Proper communication with air traffic control.
  • Human Factors: Aviation physiology, decision-making, and risk management.

FAA Knowledge Test

Prepare for and pass the FAA Private Pilot Knowledge Test.

  • Consists of 60 multiple-choice questions.
  • A minimum score of 70% is required to pass.
  • Covers all topics from the aeronautical knowledge instruction.

Flight Training Requirements 14 CFR 61.109

Minimum Flight Experience

  • Total Flight Time: At least 40 hours of flight time, which must include:
    • Dual Instruction (with an instructor): Minimum of 20 hours.
    • Solo Flight Time: Minimum of 10 hours flying solo.

Dual Flight Training Must Include

  • 3 hours of cross-country flight training.
  • 3 hours of night flying, including:
    • One cross-country flight over 100 nautical miles total distance.
    • 10 takeoffs and landings to a full stop at night.
  • 3 hours of instrument training in actual or simulated conditions.
  • 3 hours of flight training in preparation for the practical test (within two months before the test).

Solo Flight Requirements

  • 5 hours of solo cross-country flight time.
  • One solo cross-country flight of at least 150 nautical miles total distance, with full-stop landings at three points, and one segment of the flight consisting of a straight-line distance of more than 50 nautical miles between takeoff and landing locations.
  • 3 solo takeoffs and landings to a full stop at an airport with an operating control tower.

Additional Requirements

  • Age Requirement: Must be at least 17 years old (16 for glider or balloon ratings).
  • Medical Certificate: Obtain at least a Third-Class FAA Medical Certificate or qualify under BasicMed.
  • English Language Proficiency: Must be able to read, speak, write, and understand English.
  • Student Pilot Certificate: Hold a valid U.S. student pilot certificate, sport pilot certificate, or recreational pilot certificate.
